This course is demanding in terms of its reading and research assignments, yet fulfilling.
This was a helpful introduction to the historiographical debates of International History, but also to the practical processes of graduate school-- doing primary source research, selecting the scope of a paper or book topic, reviewing articles, discussing and presenting work with colleagues, the scrupulous use of sources.
If you want to know more about the globalizing tendencies and countertrends in politics, societies and cultures of the modern period and how they can be viewed, traced, interpreted and evaluated, take this course!
